Persistence is key.
Every CS majors dream is landing that 6-figure, big tech job. As we all know, this is way easier said than done. The competition grows everyday, and it becomes harder and harder to get a foot in the door. With the right preparation, however, it's possible to find yourself working at a FAANG company. This recruitment season I managed to get in the process with Meta (Facebook), Apple, Netflix, and Google. Here's how I did it.
how to land a FAANG internship.
The initial step is your resume. Make sure you experiences are tailored to the role and your bullet points show impact. Doesn't hurt to throw a little leadership experience in there if you have any.
To prepare for phone screens and to generally do well during interviews when behavioral quesitions pop up, it's a good idea to research the company you are applying to.
Leetcode-style technical questions during interviews almost an industry standard, so it's key to get as much practice with the different questions topics as you can. I recommend Neetcode's Blind 75, which gives you a solid foundation to solve further problems.
Prepare for the behavioral questions. This is where your company research will come in handy as well. Demonstrate your background knowledge of the company and ask insightful questions that show a genuine interest in the work.
